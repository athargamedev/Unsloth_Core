# Space Primer — Quick Reference for SpaceGuide NPC

## The Solar System
- **Sun**: Medium-sized yellow dwarf star. 99.8% of solar system's mass. Surface temp ~10,000°F (5,500°C).
- **Inner planets** (rocky): Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars. Small, solid surface.
- **Outer planets** (gas giants): Jupiter (largest, Great Red Spot), Saturn (rings of ice/rock), Uranus (rotates on side), Neptune (strongest winds).
- **Dwarf planets**: Pluto (reclassified 2006), Eris, Makemake, Haumea, Ceres.
- **Asteroid Belt**: Between Mars and Jupiter. Thousands of rocky bodies.
- **Kuiper Belt**: Beyond Neptune. Home to comets and dwarf planets.
- **Oort Cloud**: Distant sphere of icy bodies. Source of long-period comets.

## Stars & Galaxies
- **Life cycle of a star**: Nebula → Protostar → Main Sequence → Red Giant/Supergiant → White Dwarf/Neutron Star/Black Hole.
- **Our Sun**: Main sequence star. ~5 billion years old. ~5 billion years remaining before red giant phase.
- **Types of stars**: Red dwarfs (most common, long-lived), Yellow dwarfs (like our Sun), Blue giants (hot, short-lived), Red supergiants (massive, end in supernova).
- **Constellations**: 88 official. Patterns of stars at different distances. Used for navigation and storytelling.
- **Milky Way**: Our galaxy. Spiral galaxy. ~100,000 light-years across. ~100-400 billion stars.
- **Types of galaxies**: Spiral (Milky Way, Andromeda), Elliptical (old stars, little gas), Irregular (no defined shape).
- **Light-year**: Distance light travels in one year. ~5.88 trillion miles (9.46 trillion km).

## Space Exploration
- **Firsts**: Sputnik 1 (1957, first satellite), Yuri Gagarin (1961, first human in space), Apollo 11 (1969, first moon landing).
- **Space Shuttle** (1981-2011): Reusable spacecraft. 135 missions. Hubble Space Telescope deployed.
- **International Space Station**: Orbiting lab since 1998. 250 miles up. Orbits Earth every 90 minutes. 16 sunsets/day.
- **Mars Rovers**: Sojourner (1997), Spirit/Opportunity (2004), Curiosity (2012), Perseverance (2021). Perseverance carries Ingenuity helicopter.
- **Voyager 1 & 2**: Launched 1977. Explored outer planets. Voyager 1 entered interstellar space 2012.
- **James Webb Space Telescope**: Launched 2021. Infrared telescope. Studies early universe, exoplanet atmospheres.
- **Artemis Program**: Returning humans to the Moon. First woman and next man. Gateway lunar space station.

## Telescopes & Observation
- **Refracting telescope**: Uses lenses to bend light. Early design (Galileo). Limited by lens size.
- **Reflecting telescope**: Uses mirrors. Most large telescopes (Keck, JWST). No chromatic aberration.
- **Radio telescopes**: Detect radio waves from space. Can observe through dust. Arecibo, Very Large Array.
- **Hubble Space Telescope**: Optical telescope above atmosphere. Deep Field images show galaxies from early universe.
- **Light pollution**: Urban sky glow limits visible stars. Dark sky reserves preserve night viewing.

## Key Astronomy Concepts
- **Gravity**: Determines orbits. Stronger with more mass, decreases with distance.
- **Electromagnetic spectrum**: Radio → Microwave → Infrared → Visible → UV → X-ray → Gamma. Different telescopes see different wavelengths.
- **Redshift**: Light stretched as universe expands. More distant = more redshifted. Evidence for Big Bang.
- **Exoplanets**: Planets orbiting other stars. Discovery methods: transit (Kepler), radial velocity, direct imaging. Over 5,000 confirmed.
- **Dark matter**: Invisible matter detected by gravitational effects. Makes up ~27% of universe.
- **Dark energy**: Unknown force accelerating universal expansion. ~68% of universe.

## Notable Space Phenomena
- **Black holes**: Regions where gravity is so extreme nothing escapes. Event horizon boundary. Sagittarius A* at Milky Way's center.
- **Neutron stars**: Remnants of supernova explosions. Incredibly dense (teaspoon weighs billions of tons). Pulsars are rotating neutron stars.
- **Nebulae**: Clouds of gas and dust. Star nurseries (Orion Nebula). Remnants of dead stars (Crab Nebula).
- **Aurora**: Solar particles interacting with Earth's magnetic field. Green/red/blue lights near poles.
- **Meteor showers**: Earth passes through comet debris. Perseids (August), Geminids (December).
