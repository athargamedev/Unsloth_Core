# Fitness Coach Primer — Quick Reference for FitnessCoach NPC

## Exercise Science Fundamentals
- **Muscle Groups**: Major: chest (pectorals), back (lats, traps, rhomboids), shoulders (deltoids), arms (biceps, triceps), legs (quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, calves), core (abdominals, obliques, erector spinae).
- **Progressive Overload**: Gradually increase weight, reps, sets, or frequency. The fundamental principle for strength and muscle growth. Without it, gains plateau.
- **Repetition Ranges**: Strength (1-5 reps, heavy weight), Hypertrophy (6-12 reps, moderate weight), Endurance (15+ reps, light weight). All ranges build muscle but emphasize different adaptations.
- **Concentric vs Eccentric**: Concentric = muscle shortens under tension (lifting phase). Eccentric = muscle lengthens under tension (lowering phase). Eccentric causes more muscle damage and soreness.
- **Rest Periods**: Strength (3-5 min), Hypertrophy (60-90 sec), Endurance (30-60 sec). Longer rest = better recovery for heavy sets.
- **Time Under Tension**: Total time muscle is under load during a set. 30-60 seconds per set is typical for hypertrophy.

## Major Training Modalities
- **Strength Training**: Compound lifts (squat, deadlift, bench press, overhead press, pull-ups, rows). Focus on form, then add weight. Train 3-6 times per week depending on split.
- **Cardiovascular Training**: Zone 2 (low intensity, fat burning, base building), HIIT (high intensity intervals, VO2 max, time efficient), steady state (moderate pace, endurance). Variety is key.
- **Flexibility & Mobility**: Static stretching (held 30+ sec, after workout), dynamic stretching (active movements, before workout), foam rolling (self-myofascial release). Improves range of motion and reduces injury risk.
- **Bodyweight Training**: Push-ups, pull-ups, squats, lunges, planks, burpees. Good for home workouts, beginner conditioning, and travel.
- **Circuit Training**: Multiple exercises performed back-to-back with minimal rest. Combines strength and cardio. High calorie burn, time efficient.

## Nutrition Fundamentals
- **Macronutrients**: Protein (4 cal/g, 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight for muscle building), Carbohydrates (4 cal/g, primary fuel for exercise), Fats (9 cal/g, hormone function, health).
- **Caloric Balance**: Surplus = weight gain (muscle + some fat for bulking). Deficit = weight loss (fat + some muscle, need adequate protein). Maintenance = weight stable.
- **Protein Timing**: Distribute throughout the day (20-40g per meal). Post-workout window is real but not urgent (within 2 hours). Total daily intake matters most.
- **Hydration**: Water before, during, and after exercise. Thirst is a late indicator of dehydration. Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) important for intense or long sessions.
- **Pre-Workout Nutrition**: Simple carbs 30-60 min before (banana, oats). Protein optional. Avoid heavy fats and fiber right before training.
- **Supplements**: Protein powder (convenient), creatine monohydrate (best studied, 5g/day), caffeine (performance boost), vitamin D (if deficient). Most supplements are unnecessary.

## Common Workout Programming
- **Push/Pull/Legs**: Classic 3-day split. Push (chest, shoulders, triceps), Pull (back, biceps, rear delts), Legs (quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves). Can run 2x per week = 6 days.
- **Upper/Lower Split**: 4 days/week. Upper body day and lower body day repeated twice. Good balance of frequency and volume.
- **Full Body**: 3 days/week. Compound lifts every session. Great for beginners and time-efficient training.
- **Progressive Programs**: Starting Strength (beginner), StrongLifts 5x5, 5/3/1 (intermediate), PHUL (power hypertrophy), PPL (advanced).
- **Deload Week**: Reduce volume/intensity by 40-60% every 4-8 weeks. Allows recovery, prevents burnout, reduces injury risk.

## Safety & Form
- **Warm-Up**: 5-10 mins light cardio + dynamic stretching + warm-up sets. Increases blood flow, prepares joints, reduces injury risk.
- **Cool-Down**: 5-10 mins light activity + static stretching. Gradual heart rate reduction, improve flexibility over time.
- **Lifting Form**: Neutral spine for all lifts. Brace core before each rep. Full range of motion unless contraindicated. Control the weight — don't bounce or use momentum.
- **Common Injuries**: Lower back strain (poor form, weak core), shoulder impingement (poor pressing mechanics), knee pain (quad dominance, poor tracking). Most preventable with good form.
- **Listen to Your Body**: Sharp pain ≠ good soreness. Distinguish between muscle soreness (DOMS, 24-72h after) and acute pain (stop immediately). Rest when needed.
- **Overtraining Signs**: Persistent fatigue, poor sleep, decreased performance, mood changes, frequent illness. Take rest seriously.
