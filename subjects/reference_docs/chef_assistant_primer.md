# Chef Primer — Quick Reference for ChefAssistant NPC

## Knife Skills
- **Chef's knife**: 8-10 inch, most versatile. Grip: pinch blade between thumb and forefinger.
- **Basic cuts**: Batonnet (1/4 x 1/4 x 2-3 inch), Julienne (1/8 x 1/8), Brunoise (1/8 dice). 
- **Dice sizes**: Large (3/4), Medium (1/2), Small (1/4), Brunoise (1/8).
- **Chopping onion**: Cut off top, leave root intact. Halve through root. Peel. Horizontal cuts toward root, then vertical, then slice down.
- **Knife safety**: Curl fingertips under, knuckles guide blade. Sharp knife is safer than dull. Always cut on a stable cutting board.

## Cooking Techniques
- **Sauté**: High heat, small amount of fat, frequent tossing. For tender vegetables and small meat pieces.
- **Roast**: Dry heat in oven. Even browning. For meats, vegetables. Internal temp determines doneness.
- **Braise**: Brown then slow-cook in liquid. For tough meats (brisket, short ribs). Low temp breaks down collagen.
- **Steam**: Cook with steam above boiling water. Preserves nutrients, delicate flavors. For vegetables, fish, dumplings.
- **Poach**: Cook submerged in liquid at 160-180°F (not boiling). For eggs, fish, fruit.
- **Deep fry**: Fully submerged in hot oil. 350-375°F typical. Batters and breading create crispy crust.

## Ingredient Science
- **Maillard Reaction**: Browning reaction between amino acids and sugars at ~285°F+. Creates complex savory flavors.
- **Caramelization**: Sugar browning at ~320°F+. Sweeter than Maillard.
- **Emulsion**: Mixture of two unmixable liquids. Vinaigrette = oil + vinegar (temporary). Mayonnaise = oil + egg yolk (permanent).
- **Gluten**: Protein network in wheat flour. Develops with kneading. Creates structure in bread. Overworking makes tough baked goods.
- **Acid**: Lemon juice, vinegar. Brightens flavors, tenderizes meat, prevents browning (apples, avocado).

## Flavor Pairing Principles
- **Salt**: Enhances all flavors. Reduces bitterness. Different salts (kosher, sea, table) have different densities.
- **Fat**: Carries flavor. Butter, oil, rendered animal fat. Adds richness and mouthfeel.
- **Acid**: Balances richness. Brightens dishes. Often missing in home cooking.
- **Heat**: Spices (chili, pepper). Use with fat for better distribution.
- **Herbs**: Delicate (fresh added at end) vs robust (dried added early). Parsley, basil, thyme, rosemary, oregano.

## Food Safety
- **Temperature Danger Zone**: 40-140°F (4-60°C). Bacteria multiply rapidly. Don't leave food out over 2 hours.
- **Minimum internal temps**: Poultry 165°F, Ground meats 160°F, Steaks 145°F, Fish 145°F.
- **Cross-contamination**: Separate cutting boards for raw meat and produce. Wash hands between handling different foods.
- **Storage**: Raw meat on bottom shelf. Cooked foods above. FIFO = First In, First Out.
- **Refusal pattern**: If asked for crash diets, eating-disorder advice, or unsafe food hacks, refuse clearly and redirect to balanced meals, portion control, safe temperatures, or technique basics.

## Mother Sauces (Classic French)
1. **Béchamel**: Milk + white roux (butter + flour). Base for cream sauces.
2. **Velouté**: Light stock + blond roux. Base for poultry/fish sauces.
3. **Espagnole**: Brown stock + brown roux + tomato. Base for meat sauces.
4. **Tomate**: Tomato-based. Modern version is crushed tomatoes.
5. **Hollandaise**: Egg yolk + clarified butter + lemon juice. Emulsion sauce.

## Kitchen Organization (Mise en Place)
- **Mise en place**: "Everything in its place." Prep all ingredients before cooking.
- **Kitchen hierarchy**: Chef → Sous Chef → Line Cooks → Prep Cooks.
- **Tools**: Chef's knife, paring knife, serrated knife, cutting board, tongs, spatula, ladle, whisk, colander, measuring cups.
