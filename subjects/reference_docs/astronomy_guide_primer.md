# Astronomy Guide Primer — Quick Reference for AstronomyGuide NPC

## Solar System
- **Sun**: G2V type yellow dwarf star. Diameter 1.39M km. Surface temp ~5500°C. Contains 99.86% of solar system mass. Fusion converts hydrogen to helium.
- **Terrestrial Planets**: Mercury (no atmosphere, extreme temps), Venus (runaway greenhouse, 462°C surface), Earth (liquid water, life), Mars (thin CO2 atmosphere, Olympus Mons largest volcano).
- **Gas Giants**: Jupiter (largest, Great Red Spot storm, 95 Earth-mass), Saturn (rings, density less than water, 95 Earth-mass).
- **Ice Giants**: Uranus (rotates on its side, -224°C), Neptune (fastest winds in solar system, 2100 km/h).
- **Dwarf Planets**: Pluto (reclassified 2006), Eris, Haumea, Makemake, Ceres. Defined by not clearing orbital neighborhood.
- **Asteroid Belt**: Between Mars and Jupiter. Ceres is largest. Source of most meteorites.
- **Kuiper Belt & Oort Cloud**: Icy bodies beyond Neptune. Oort Cloud is spherical shell, source of long-period comets.

## Stars & Stellar Evolution
- **Star Formation**: Clouds of gas and dust (nebulae) collapse under gravity. Protostar forms, then nuclear fusion ignites.
- **Main Sequence**: Stars fuse hydrogen to helium. Mass determines lifespan (massive = shorter). Our Sun: ~10 billion years total.
- **Red Giant**: Hydrogen depleted, core contracts, outer layers expand. Our Sun will become a red giant in ~5 billion years.
- **Stellar Death**: Low-mass stars → white dwarf. Massive stars → supernova → neutron star or black hole.
- **Types**: OBAFGKM classification (hottest to coolest). Our Sun is G2 type.
- **Binary Stars**: ~50% of stars are in binary or multi-star systems. Important for measuring stellar masses.

## Galaxies & Cosmology
- **Milky Way**: Barred spiral galaxy, ~100,000 light-years diameter, ~100-400 billion stars. Contains a supermassive black hole (Sagittarius A*).
- **Galaxy Types**: Spiral (milky way), elliptical (old stars, little gas), irregular (no defined shape).
- **Light-Year**: Distance light travels in one year — ~9.46 trillion km. Proxima Centauri is 4.24 light-years away.
- **Redshift & Expansion**: Universe is expanding. Distant galaxies show redshift. Hubble's Law: farther = faster recession.
- **Big Bang**: Universe began ~13.8 billion years ago. Cosmic microwave background radiation is afterglow evidence.
- **Dark Matter & Dark Energy**: Dark matter (~27% of universe) inferred from gravitational effects. Dark energy (~68%) drives accelerating expansion.

## Observational Astronomy
- **Telescopes**: Refractors (lenses), reflectors (mirrors). Larger aperture = more light gathering = fainter objects visible.
- **Major Observatories**: Hubble Space Telescope (visible/UV), James Webb Space Telescope (infrared), Chandra (X-ray).
- **Constellations**: 88 official constellations. Patterns of stars that appear close from Earth but may be far apart in space.
- **Messier Objects**: 110 deep-sky objects catalogued by Charles Messier. Galaxies, nebulae, star clusters.
- **Magnitude Scale**: Brightness scale. Lower numbers = brighter. Each magnitude step = ~2.5x brightness difference. Sirius is -1.46.
- **Phases of Moon**: New → Waxing Crescent → First Quarter → Waxing Gibbous → Full → Waning Gibbous → Last Quarter → Waning Crescent.

## Space Exploration
- **Firsts**: Sputnik (1957, first satellite), Yuri Gagarin (1961, first human in space), Apollo 11 (1969, Moon landing).
- **Major Missions**: Voyager (outer planets + interstellar, 1977), Hubble (1990-), ISS (1998-), Mars rovers (Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, Perseverance).
- **Rocketry**: Chemical rockets (most common), ion thrusters (efficient), reusable rockets (SpaceX Falcon 9).
- **Human Spaceflight**: ISS orbits at ~400 km altitude. Future plans: Moon (Artemis program), Mars. Challenges: radiation, isolation, life support.

## Key Concepts
- **Gravity**: Determines orbits. Newton's law: F = G*m1*m2/r². Einstein: gravity is curvature of spacetime.
- **Electromagnetic Spectrum**: Radio, microwave, infrared, visible, ultraviolet, X-ray, gamma. Each reveals different cosmic phenomena.
- **Habitable Zone**: Distance from star where liquid water can exist. Earth is in Sun's habitable zone.
- **Exoplanets**: Planets around other stars. Discovery methods: transit (Kepler), radial velocity, direct imaging. 5000+ confirmed.
- **Nuclear Fusion**: Sun fuses H→He in core at ~15M°C. Mass converted to energy via E=mc².
- **Seasons**: Caused by Earth's axial tilt (23.5°), not distance from Sun.

## Safety Boundaries & Misconceptions
- **Astrology boundary**: AstronomyGuide explains astronomy as science and does not claim star signs predict personality, health, or future events.
- **Conspiracy refusal**: Redirect Moon-landing denial, flat-Earth claims, and "NASA hiding evidence" claims toward observable evidence, missions, and measurement methods.
- **Aliens boundary**: If asked whether aliens exist or what they look like, refuse speculation and redirect to exoplanet searches, biosignatures, and the limits of current evidence.
- **Scale misconception**: Planets and stars are often shown too close together in diagrams; real solar system distances are much larger.
- **Season misconception**: Seasons come from axial tilt, not Earth being closer or farther from the Sun.
- **Black hole misconception**: Black holes do not vacuum up everything nearby; objects can orbit them like other massive bodies if outside the event horizon.
- **Refusal pattern**: State the evidence boundary, decline the unsupported claim, and offer a concrete science-first alternative.
