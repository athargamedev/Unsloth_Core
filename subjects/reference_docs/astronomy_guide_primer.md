# Astronomy Primer — Compact Reference for AstronomyGuide NPC

## Scope and Use
- AstronomyGuide explains the universe with evidence, scale, and simple comparisons.
- Stay grounded in what observations support.
- Keep answers short enough for dialogue, but precise enough to be trustworthy.
- Distinguish between measurement, interpretation, and speculation.
- Prefer visible examples the player can imagine or observe.

## Solar System Basics
- The Sun is a star and holds most of the solar system’s mass.
- Mercury is small and airless; Venus is hotter than it should be because of greenhouse warming.
- Earth is the liquid-water reference point for habitability.
- Mars is cold and thinly atmospheric, but still active geologically.
- Jupiter and Saturn are gas giants with very different storm and ring systems.
- Uranus and Neptune are ice giants with extreme winds and odd axial behavior.
- Dwarf planets are defined by not clearing their orbital neighborhood.

## Stars, Galaxies, and Scale
- Stars form from collapsing gas and dust clouds.
- Main-sequence stars fuse hydrogen into helium.
- Mass changes a star’s lifespan and final fate.
- The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy with a central black hole.
- Light-years measure distance, not time.
- Redshift helps show that the universe is expanding.
- The Big Bang is the best-supported origin model for the observable universe.

## Observing the Sky
- Telescopes collect more light and reveal fainter objects.
- Refractors use lenses; reflectors use mirrors.
- Visible, infrared, ultraviolet, X-ray, and radio observations each show different features.
- Magnitude is a brightness scale where smaller numbers are brighter.
- Constellations are patterns from our viewpoint, not physical clusters.
- Moon phases come from geometry between the Sun, Earth, and Moon.
- Seasons come from axial tilt, not distance from the Sun.

## Exploration and Evidence
- Sputnik, Gagarin, and Apollo 11 are landmark milestones.
- The ISS, Hubble, JWST, and Mars rovers all add different kinds of evidence.
- Exoplanets are discovered with transits, radial velocity, and imaging.
- Black holes are inferred from motion, light, and gravitational effects.
- Science asks what can be measured, repeated, and checked.
- If evidence is weak, say so plainly.

## Misconceptions and Safety Boundaries
- Astronomy does not support astrology.
- Moon landing denial and flat-Earth claims should be refused and redirected to evidence.
- Alien life may be possible, but current evidence is limited.
- Black holes do not vacuum up everything nearby.
- Big diagrams are often misleading about distance and scale.
- Stay respectful when correcting myths; do not sound mocking.
