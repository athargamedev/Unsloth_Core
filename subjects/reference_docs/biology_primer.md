# Biology Primer — Quick Reference for BiologyTutor NPC

## Cell Biology
- **Cell theory**: (1) All living things are made of cells. (2) Cells are the basic unit of life. (3) All cells come from pre-existing cells.
- **Prokaryotic cells**: No nucleus or membrane-bound organelles (bacteria, archaea). Smaller, simpler.
- **Eukaryotic cells**: Have nucleus and organelles (plants, animals, fungi, protists). Larger, more complex.
- **Organelles**: Nucleus (DNA), mitochondria (energy/ATP), ribosomes (protein synthesis), ER (protein/lipid processing), Golgi (packaging), lysosomes (digestion), chloroplasts (photosynthesis — plants only).
- **Cell membrane**: Phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins. Selectively permeable — controls what enters/exits.

## Genetics & DNA
- **DNA structure**: Double helix. Nucleotides = phosphate + sugar (deoxyribose) + nitrogen base (A, T, C, G).
- **Base pairing**: A–T (2 bonds), C–G (3 bonds).
- **Central dogma**: DNA → RNA → Protein. Transcription (DNA → mRNA in nucleus), Translation (mRNA → protein at ribosomes).
- **Genes**: Segments of DNA that code for proteins. Humans have ~20,000 protein-coding genes.
- **Mutations**: Changes in DNA sequence. Types: substitution, insertion, deletion. Effects vary from silent to harmful.

## Evolution & Natural Selection
- **Natural selection**: Individuals with traits better suited to the environment survive and reproduce more. Three requirements: variation, heritability, differential reproduction.
- **Speciation**: New species form when populations become reproductively isolated (geographic or behavioral barriers).
- **Evidence**: Fossil record, homologous structures, DNA sequence similarity, vestigial organs, direct observation (antibiotic resistance).
- **Common misconception**: Evolution does NOT mean organisms "try" to adapt. Variation exists first; selection acts on it.

## Ecology
- **Levels**: organism → population → community → ecosystem → biome → biosphere.
- **Energy flow**: Sun → producers (autotrophs) → consumers (heterotrophs) → decomposers. ~10% energy transferred per trophic level.
- **Biogeochemical cycles**: Water cycle (evaporation/precipitation), carbon cycle (photosynthesis/respiration), nitrogen cycle (fixation/nitrification/denitrification).
- **Carrying capacity**: Maximum population size an environment can sustain. Limited by resources, predation, disease.

## Human Body Systems
- **Circulatory**: Heart pumps blood through arteries/veins/capillaries. Carries O₂, nutrients, waste.
- **Respiratory**: Lungs exchange O₂ and CO₂ via diffusion in alveoli.
- **Nervous**: Neurons transmit electrical signals. Brain + spinal cord = central nervous system.
- **Immune**: Innate (skin, inflammation) and adaptive (antibodies, T-cells, memory). Vaccines train adaptive immunity.
- **Homeostasis**: Maintaining stable internal conditions (temperature, pH, blood sugar). Negative feedback loops.

## Classification & Diversity
- **Domains**: Bacteria, Archaea, Eukarya.
- **Kingdoms**: Animalia (animals), Plantae (plants), Fungi, Protista, Monera (bacteria).
- **Binomial nomenclature**: Genus species (e.g., *Homo sapiens*). Genus capitalized, species lowercase, both italicized.
- **Biodiversity**: Variety of life at genetic, species, and ecosystem levels. Threatened by habitat loss, climate change, pollution.
